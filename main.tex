\section{About these templates}

These templates are designed to provide a starting point for ANU Research Ethics attachments in Latex. This is particularly useful for computing students more familiar with collaborating in Latex (e.g., using Overleaf) than Microsoft Word.

The templates use a reasonable version of ANU style as required for official documents. The main font is Public Sans and the official ANU logo is applied to the header.

N.B., these templates \emph{must} be read alongside the ANU ethics documentation, including, but not limited to:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \href{https://services.anu.edu.au/research-support/research-ethics-integrity-compliance/getting-human-research-ethics-approval}{Getting Human Ethics Approval (link)}

    \item \href{https://services.anu.edu.au/research-support/research-ethics-integrity-compliance/getting-human-research-ethics-approval/before-you-begin}{"Before you begin" (link)}

    \item \href{https://services.anu.edu.au/research-support/research-ethics-integrity-compliance/getting-human-research-ethics-approval/participant-information-sheet-consent}{Participant Information Sheet \& Consent (link)}
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Styling the documents}

You will have to modify the header title and subtitle to make sure it fits within one line.

\paragraph{Template files}

I've included files for each attachment you are likely to need.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \verb|project_description.tex| (required in \textbf{all} applications)
    \item \verb|participant_information_sheet.tex| (required in \textbf{all} applications)
    \item \verb|consent_form.tex| (required in \textbf{all} applications)
    \item \verb|sample_questions.tex| (required if you are using interview or survey methods)
    \item \verb|recruitment_resources.tex| (required if you are recruiting participants)
\end{enumerate}

In a normal HCI study with indirect recruitment and interviews you will need five attachments corresponding to these files in your ethics application.

The templates here include sample content, you can view examples of previous research ethics applications in our SMCClab Team shared folders.
